Executive Summary

The data the robots need does not exist. Except here.

The race to build machines that operate in human spaces has entered its decisive phase. Tesla is manufacturing humanoid robots for home deployment by 2027. NVIDIA is building world foundation models. Apple confirmed development of home robotics products. The embodied AI market is projected to exceed $23 billion by 2030, growing at 39% annually.

Every one of these programs shares a common bottleneck: the absence of large-scale, high-fidelity training data captured from real residential environments. Synthetic platforms consistently fail to replicate the photometric complexity, spatial irregularity, and material diversity of actual homes. The sim-to-real gap remains the primary barrier between laboratory demonstrations and reliable domestic deployment.

EmbodiedNet is the dataset that closes this gap. Built over six years of continuous professional real estate media capture, it comprises interior RAW HDR brackets, ego-perspective 4K motion video in cinema-grade log profiles, exterior stills, aerial footage, and metric floor plans across 500+ unique residential properties.

The companies building the robots exist. The capital to fund them exists. The models to train them exist. What does not exist, anywhere in the commercial or academic ecosystem, is a large-scale, high-fidelity, multi-modal corpus of real residential environments suitable for training domestic AI systems.

The Synthetic Data Problem

Simulation does not work well enough

Synthetic data generation is the prevailing approach to training embodied AI. Platforms like NVIDIA Isaac Sim, Meta AI Habitat, and Google's simulation tools generate massive volumes of virtual training data. The appeal is obvious: infinite scalability and full control. The problem is equally well-documented.

Photometric Divergence
Synthetic lighting distributions, material reflectances, and shadow behaviors differ systematically from real sensor captures.
Geometric Oversimplification
Idealized 3D meshes lack the surface irregularity, clutter density, and spatial variation of actual rooms.
Sensor Artifact Absence
Real cameras produce noise, lens distortion, depth quantization errors, and ambient light interference that simulators omit.
Distribution Shift
Object arrangements, room proportions, and material combinations in synthetic environments fail to match real residential distributions.

Why homes are uniquely difficult to simulate

Industrial environments have regularities that simulation can approximate: standardized racking, uniform lighting, predictable floors. Residential environments have none of these regularities.

A single home contains dozens of distinct material surfaces: hardwood transitioning to tile to carpet, granite adjacent to stainless steel, fabric next to glass. Natural light shifts throughout the day and across seasons, producing dynamic shadows and extreme luminance contrasts. Furniture arrangements are idiosyncratic. Rooms serve multiple overlapping functions.

Generating a synthetic residential environment at sufficient fidelity requires artists, 3D modelers, material scientists, and lighting engineers working for weeks per home. Generating 500 such environments is a project measured in years and tens of millions of dollars, with no guarantee of closing the transfer gap.

EmbodiedNet bypasses the synthesis problem entirely. It provides the ground truth that synthetic pipelines are trying to approximate, captured at native sensor fidelity across 500+ unique residential environments.

Structural Irreplaceability

Why this cannot be rebuilt

The critical question any prospective acquirer will ask is not whether this is good data. The question is: why can we not produce this ourselves? The answer operates across multiple independent dimensions, each a barrier that money alone cannot overcome.

Market Window Permanently Closed

The residential market between 2020 and 2023 was anomalous by any historical measure. Post-COVID demand migration, historically low mortgage rates, rapid new construction, and elevated agent marketing budgets drove transaction volume and listing density to levels not seen before or since. The economic environment that funded EmbodiedNet is a historical artifact.

Residential Access Cannot Be Recreated

The 500+ properties were privately owned homes. Access was granted under commercial photography agreements at time of listing. Those homes have since been sold. Owners have changed. Interior configurations have been modified. The specific combination of furnishings, staging, light conditions, and seasonal context no longer exists at any of these addresses.

Temporal Diversity Cannot Be Fast-Tracked

Four full seasonal cycles across six years means the corpus contains every seasonal lighting condition and six years of evolving design conventions: color trends, fixture styles, furnishing density, staging philosophies, and landscaping choices. A purpose-built capture sprint of any duration shorter than years produces a single seasonal slice.

Clean IP Chain of Title

All intellectual property is original to the collector. No data has been scraped, licensed from third parties, or generated synthetically. The corpus has never been licensed to any entity. This clean chain of title eliminates licensing encumbrance risks and enables exclusive acquisition structures that provide competitive advantage.
