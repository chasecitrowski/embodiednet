The Measurement Gap

The field can train models. It cannot tell you if they are correct.

Embodied AI has converged on a powerful training loop: build synthetic environments, generate massive volumes of simulated experience, train models at scale. The simulation infrastructure is impressive and improving. ProcTHOR generates 10,000+ procedural homes. Isaac Sim provides physics-accurate dynamics. Habitat runs at thousands of frames per second.

But when those models leave simulation and enter real homes, performance degrades in ways that are difficult to isolate, quantify, or correct. The field calls this the sim-to-real gap. Every lab acknowledges it. No lab can systematically measure it.

The response has been to make simulation more realistic: better renderers, better physics, more procedural diversity. This is valuable work. But it operates without a reference. There is no structured, real-world ground truth against which to evaluate whether those improvements actually close the gap. Labs deploy, observe failures, return to sim, adjust, and deploy again. This is not calibration. It is trial and error with expensive real-world deployments as the feedback signal.

Synthetic training environments are powerful and improving. But without a real-world reference plane, no lab can answer the most basic question about their own models: how far is this from reality?

What Does Not Exist

Simulation is valuable. But without a reference, it is unmeasurable.

The current embodied AI stack has four layers: synthetic environments, training pipelines, model outputs, and deployment. Between model outputs and deployment, there is no consistent real-world reference for validation.

No Photometric Reference
No dataset captures real residential lighting at native sensor encoding across hundreds of homes. Labs cannot measure the gap between their renderer output and real sensor response.
No Geometric Ground Truth at Scale
Existing real-world scans are mesh reconstructions, not native captures. They lose the sensor artifacts, noise profiles, and encoding characteristics that deployed systems will encounter.
No Motion Continuity Baseline
Static scans cannot validate navigation models. Calibration requires ego-perspective traversals through real residential layouts with preserved room-to-room continuity.
No Residential Reference at All
Every existing dataset mixes residential with commercial, office, and institutional spaces. None is 100% real homes. Deployed systems will operate in homes. The reference layer should reflect that.

Why residential environments specifically

The places where embodied AI will be deployed at consumer scale are homes. Not warehouses, not factories, not purpose-built test environments. Homes.

Residential spaces are uniquely resistant to simulation. A single home contains dozens of distinct material surfaces, dynamic natural lighting that shifts with time and season, idiosyncratic furniture arrangements, and spatial layouts that follow no standard template. The variation across 500 homes in a single metro region exceeds what any procedural generator has demonstrated.

More importantly, residential spaces are where the safety and liability stakes are highest. A robot in a warehouse operates around trained personnel in controlled conditions. A robot in a home operates around children, pets, elderly occupants, and unfamiliar layouts. The tolerance for uncalibrated performance is zero.

EmbodiedNet does not replace simulation. Synthetic training is powerful and will always be used. EmbodiedNet provides the reference plane that tells you whether your simulation is close enough.

The Calibration Layer

Built to measure.
Not to train.

EmbodiedNet was not designed to be the best training dataset. It was designed to be the reference against which all training datasets and simulation environments can be measured. That is a different design objective, and it produced a different artifact. Six co-captured modalities, each preserved at native sensor fidelity.

Int. RAW Stills ARW / 0 EV

Sony ARW per room, uncompressed. ~50 final delivered stills per property, each composed from 5+ bracketed exposures, yielding 250+ individual RAW files for interiors alone.

Measures: photometric gap between synthetic renderers and real sensor response

Int. Motion S-Log3 4K

4K ego-perspective motion capture through residential spaces. S-Log3, ungraded. Segments vary by property layout and room sequence. 14mm geometry.

Measures: navigation model accuracy against real residential traversals

Brackets + HDR ARW x6 + TIFF

5-bracket auto exposure + ambient flash per anchor room. 32-bit TIFF HDR merge also delivered. Full luminance range.

Measures: dynamic range handling across the full luminance envelope

Ext. RAW Stills DNG / D-Log M

DJI DNG frames covering front, rear, sides, neighborhood context, and lot geometry. Coverage is comprehensive and adapted to each property's layout and features.

Measures: approach-to-entry spatial accuracy for delivery and service robots

Aerial Motion HEVC D-Log M

D-Log M ungraded aerial motion clip. Elevation variation, exterior geometry, neighborhood spatial context.

Measures: large-scale spatial model accuracy at property and neighborhood level

Floor Plans Metric

Labeled metric plans for both floors with room dimensions. Provides spatial scaffolding for sensor data.

Measures: reconstructed geometry against physically measured room dimensions

Structural Irreplaceability

Why this cannot be rebuilt

The critical question is not whether this is good data. The question is: why can this calibration layer not be replicated? The answer operates across multiple independent dimensions, each a barrier that money alone cannot overcome.

Market Window Permanently Closed

The residential market between 2020 and 2023 was anomalous by any historical measure. Post-COVID demand migration, historically low mortgage rates, rapid new construction, and elevated agent marketing budgets drove transaction volume and listing density to levels not seen before or since. The economic environment that funded EmbodiedNet is a historical artifact.

Residential Access Cannot Be Recreated

The 500+ properties were privately owned homes. Access was granted under commercial photography agreements at time of listing. Those homes have since been sold. Owners have changed. Interior configurations have been modified. The specific combination of furnishings, staging, light conditions, and seasonal context no longer exists at any of these addresses.

Temporal Diversity Cannot Be Fast-Tracked

Four full seasonal cycles across six years means the corpus contains every seasonal lighting condition and six years of evolving design conventions: color trends, fixture styles, furnishing density, staging philosophies, and landscaping choices. A purpose-built capture sprint of any duration shorter than years produces a single seasonal slice.

Clean IP Chain of Title

All intellectual property is original to the collector. No data has been scraped, licensed from third parties, or generated synthetically. The corpus has never been licensed to any entity. This clean chain of title eliminates licensing encumbrance risks and ensures the provenance required for a measurement reference.

Why Now

The systems are moving toward homes. The measurement layer does not exist.

Humanoid robots are entering production. Foundation models are being trained for physical interaction. Multiple programs across industry and academia are targeting residential deployment within the next two to three years. The simulation infrastructure to train these systems exists and is scaling rapidly. The measurement infrastructure to validate them in real residential environments does not. That is the gap EmbodiedNet was built to fill.

What Calibration Makes Possible

The questions you can finally answer

01
Measure the Sim-to-Real Gap

Run the same evaluation protocol against synthetic environments and EmbodiedNet properties. Quantify where and how much performance degrades. Replace guesswork with per-dimension error measurement.

02
Calibrate Model Outputs

Align spatial predictions, navigation policies, or perception outputs with real-world structure at native sensor fidelity. Not tone-mapped derivatives. Not reconstructed meshes. Real sensor data.

03
Detect Failure Modes

Identify where models break in real environments before deployment. Mixed lighting, narrow hallways, reflective surfaces, high dynamic range scenes, cluttered rooms, unusual layouts. All invisible in simulation.

04
Validate Architectures

Isolate whether a performance difference comes from the model architecture, the training data, or the sim-to-real distribution gap itself. Controlled comparison across hundreds of real environments.

05
Study Graceful Degradation

Train with the full modality stack, then systematically withhold modalities to measure how gracefully performance degrades. Mirrors real deployment: a robot entering an unseen home has no prior data of any kind.

06
Benchmark Reproducibly

VB1 results are independently reproducible using open-source tools. Any lab can run COLMAP on the same input and compare. Frozen, property-disjoint evaluation slices ensure consistent benchmarking.

Training inputs. None designed as calibration.
